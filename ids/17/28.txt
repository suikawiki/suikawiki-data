*INTRODUCTION

Army ant colonies are among the largest and most cohesive societies. Individual
ants are typically less than 10 mm long, communication between them is
restricted to local chemical and tactile stimuli, and yet their foraging systems
coordinate hundreds of thousands of individuals and cover some 1000 m 2 in a
day (Fig. 1) (Raignier and Van Boven, 1955; Rettenmeyer, 1963; Schneirla,
1971; Topoff, 1972; Gotwald, 1982; Franks and Fletcher, 1983; Burton and
Franks, 1985). Such swarm raids pose in the strongest possible way the general
problem of collective decision making without any form of centralized control.
Our model shows how their characteristic patterns could be self-organizing,
i.e., generated from the interactions between many identical foragers, each with
simple trail-laying and trail-following behavior.
